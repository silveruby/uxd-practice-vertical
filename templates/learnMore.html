UX Design Practice Verticals

A snapshot of the activities that are necessary to architect and design human-computer interactions

My story

As a UX practitioner, I have wore different hats for many projects. Sometimes I work directly with stakeholders in high-level meetings. And sometimes I am heads down behind my computer fixing away front-end bugs. One of my friend is a content strategist and clients often confuse her role to an editor. I think the story I am sharing with you is the story of many UX partitioners. In a world full of clients looking for UX unicorns, it’s challenging to find right UX work.

Goal of the Site

The goal of the site is to share the practice verticals with everyone who wants to define their UX path. In my journey to define my UX path, I came across an article called UX Design Practice Verticals. It breaks down UX practices into 8 main practice verticals. Each practice verticals has six area of interests, with each one building on top of the previous. I think it’s one of the most clear and structured description about UX design, taking into consideration of all the activities and roles involved in the process.
